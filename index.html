Hi, I'm Shabnam!

I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in the Quantitative and Computational Biology program at Princeton. My interests lie broadly in exploring developmental biology and collective cellular behaviours through modelling.

I completed my B.Tech. at IIT Bombay, where I did my bachelor's thesis with Prof. Ganesh Viswanathan. I spent the majority of my undergraduate years as part of Prof. Shaon Chakrabarti's lab in the Simons Centre at the National Centre for Biological Sciences, where I developed ODeGP. I have also been fortunate to work with Prof. Sandip Kar at IIT Bombay, Dr. Anton Goloborodko at the Vienna BioCenter, and Prof. Rong Li at the Mechanobiology Institute. Prior to joining Princeton, I also spent a year at Microsoft as a software engineer.

When not working, you may find me taking a long walk, playing Scrabble online, or chopping garlic and onions. I also love to dance, and am trained in Bharatanatyam. Please feel free to get in touch!
